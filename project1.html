NUMERO UNO

AN INTERACTIVE APPLICATION

TEAM

Terence Liu
Bhavin Solanki
Jessie Li
Megan Fawkes

SKILLS

Arduino
Processing
Sensors

PROBLEM

We found that it was difficult to find complete information on the Trans Mountain Pipeline expansion, as it required us to scour all over the internet in order to piece together bits of data. To solve this issue, we developed a website that included perspectives and affects it would have on stakeholders.

DESCRIPTION

This project is an interactive toy aimed at helping children learn basic mathematics, by inserting numbers into its respective slots... The toy uses force sensors to detect when a number has been inserted into the slot, a proximity sensor to turn on/off the toy when someone is within 40cm, and touch sensors to allow users to select the level of difficulty.

PROCESS

We first searched for a complex environmental issue that has not been well documented, so we decided to focus on the Trans Mountain Pipeline Expansion... Afterwards, we began researching information on the possible impact the expansion would have on the community, the industry, and the environment. After gathering the information, we began content writing, and creating infographics to display the information in an easier to understand way. After content writing we began developing a website with HTML/CSS and got constant feedback from our professor on how we could improve, such as including viewpoints from different members of the community. After finalizing our content, we began to make our website fully responsive.

ROLE

In this project, I was responsible for the overall art direction of the website, as I picked the typeface, color palette, and designed the layout. I was in charge of creating the wireframe on Sketch... I created layouts for multiple devices ranging from a desktop computer to a mobile device. I worked with my partner to code the HTML, and laid out the basic structure of the website, afterwards I took on the task of coding the CSS so that it would follow the original art direction.

REFLECTION

During this project, we had to constantly think about the perspective of the user, and areas that the user may misinterpret and break the toy... Throughout this process, I learned to think more empathetically and take into account how the user may think compared to myself. Designing for others, especially children is fun, but is also a challenging task, because they are constantly thinking outside of the box and try to twist the rules so that the game can be even more interesting. An emphasis on brainstorming made me realize that no project will succeed if the designer does not take the time to see through the eyes of the user.
